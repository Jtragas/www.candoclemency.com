Our Mission

Justice Through Clemency

CAN-DO Foundation educates the public about the collateral damage of the conspiracy law and advocates for people serving extreme sentences for nonviolent offenses.

Until current drug laws change, there will be a steady stream of people serving decades behind bars. An educated public is the first step toward real justice reform.

Founder

Amy Ralston Povah

Advocate · Filmmaker · Conspiracy Law Expert · Clinton Clemency · Trump Full Pardon

At 28, Amy was a successful VP of Sales with no criminal record. In 1989, federal agents demanded she wear a wire against her husband or face 20-to-life. She refused.

The kingpin — her husband — got 4 years in Germany. Amy received 24 years for collecting bail money. Six co-defendants swore she was not involved.

A Glamour Magazine exposé ignited national outcry. 17 members of Congress wrote in support. On July 7, 2000, President Clinton commuted her sentence.

Post-release, Amy founded CAN-DO and has helped free over 138 people. In January 2021, President Trump granted her a full pardon. In March 2025, she testified before the U.S. Sentencing Commission.
