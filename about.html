About Amy

From Conspiracy to Clemency to Pardon

At 28, Amy was a successful VP of Sales at Commonwealth Bancorp — a legitimate mortgage company owned by her then-husband Sandy Pofahl. She had no criminal record and no knowledge that Sandy was secretly manufacturing MDMA overseas.

In 1989, federal agents raided her home. They demanded she wear a wire against Sandy or face conspiracy charges carrying 20 years to life. She refused — she genuinely didn't know enough to cooperate. The agents promised to "destroy her life."

Two years later, she was indicted in Waco, TX. Denied bail. Held one year with a public defender. The kingpin — her husband — got 4 years in Germany and 3 years U.S. probation. Amy received 24 years, 4 months for collecting bail money at his request. Six co-defendants later filed affidavits swearing Amy was not involved.

From her cell at FCI Dublin, she launched a letter-writing campaign that changed everything. A landmark Glamour Magazine exposé ignited public outcry. 17 members of Congress wrote in support. On July 7, 2000, President Clinton commuted her sentence. Read the full case details →
